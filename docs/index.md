# About

*Have you ever wondered what that synth pop music was playing in the queue for the Autopia? By the way, did you know that a lot of music from the PeopleMover used to play there when it was sponsored by Chevron? And what music played on the PeopleMover anyways? And why on earth did I also hear that same music while waiting ~~at LAX~~ in line for Superstar Limo?*

*What about those Beach Boys tunes that played in Paradise Pier and sounded like they were all played on an organ? Now that I think of it, I also sure heard a lot of Beach Boys music at Sunshine Plaza, PCH Grill, and Pizza Oom Mow Mow… 🤔*

If you have asked yourself these questions, or had a remotely similar thought about music at Disneyland, you’ve come to the right place!

---

This site was created to house track lists of background music (“BGM”) from the Disneyland Resort, namely Disneyland Park, Disney(’s) California Adventure, and the Resort hotels. These lists have been posted in forums and blog posts around the internet (see [Helpful Sources](https://www.notion.so/Helpful-Sources-d8da13a8992b42aaae0f9ca8ee581128?pvs=21)). I’ve also done quite a bit of research myself, and [archived my MouseBits posts](https://www.notion.so/MouseBits-Posts-Archive-9901155c0ffe400e8ce668e330c3eee5?pvs=21) on this site. With the help of several knowledgeable and kind friends, I have tried my best to aggregate **accurate and validated** tracklists in one location for reference purposes.

So with that said, the databases for [Disneyland](https://www.notion.so/9d9b369bfdc3439fb410ecd899ae5c5f?pvs=21), [Disney(’s) California Adventure](https://www.notion.so/0adfe0d6d46d45d2acfa7d75b0b366b3?pvs=21), the [Disneyland Main Gates](https://www.notion.so/21887d69fe8d4f0fadddd68de700545e?pvs=21), the [Resort Esplanade](https://www.notion.so/21887d69fe8d4f0fadddd68de700545e?pvs=21), the [Resort Hotels](https://www.notion.so/21887d69fe8d4f0fadddd68de700545e?pvs=21), and the [Downtown Disney District](https://www.notion.so/21887d69fe8d4f0fadddd68de700545e?pvs=21) are all available to peruse. If you prefer not to use the built-in Notion viewer, you can [download the databases](https://www.notion.so/Database-Archive-cdb0fe5145f941b98616a9ed2898336b?pvs=21) and explore them offline with your tabular viewer of choice.
